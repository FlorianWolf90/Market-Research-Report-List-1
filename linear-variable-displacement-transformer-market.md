<p><p>A Linear Variable Displacement Transformer (LVDT) is an electromechanical device that converts linear displacement into an electrical signal. It operates on the principle of electromagnetic induction and provides high accuracy and precision in various applications, including aerospace, industrial automation, and robotics. The LVDT features a movable core that alters the inductance of the primary and secondary coils, leading to precise measurements of position and displacement.</p><p>The LVDT market is poised for significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for automation in industries and advancements in sensor technology. DC LVDTs, on the other hand, use direct current, offering simplicity in circuit design and stability in output, suitable for less demanding environments. Both types are utilized across various sectors, including aerospace, automotive, and manufacturing, for precise linear motion measurement and control.</p></p>
