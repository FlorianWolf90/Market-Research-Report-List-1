<p><p>Phenolic resins used in the photoresist market are categorized mainly into flake and block types. Flake phenolic resins are produced in thin, flat pieces, allowing for easy dissolution in solvents, making them suitable for high-resolution photolithography applications. Block phenolic resins, on the other hand, are denser and thicker, offering superior mechanical strength and thermal stability. Both types serve critical roles in the semiconductor industry, contributing to the precision and durability required in advanced manufacturing processes.</p></p>
