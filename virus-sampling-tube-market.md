<p><p>Virus sampling tubes are categorized into two types based on their treatment of samples: inactivated and non-inactivated. Inactivated virus sampling tubes contain agents that neutralize active viruses, making them safe for handling and transport while preserving the sample's integrity for testing. In contrast, non-inactivated tubes maintain the virus in its active state, allowing for the study of viral behavior and characteristics. Both types are essential for diagnostics, research, and public health monitoring, serving distinct purposes in virology.</p></p>
