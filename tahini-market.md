<p><p>The tahini market comprises two main types: hulled and unhulled tahini. Hulled tahini is made from sesame seeds with the outer hull removed, resulting in a smoother texture and milder flavor, making it popular for culinary applications. In contrast, unhulled tahini retains the seed's outer layer, offering a more robust taste and higher nutritional content, particularly fiber and minerals. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and dietary needs, influencing their respective market demand and applications.</p></p>
