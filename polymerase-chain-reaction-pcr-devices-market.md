<p><p>The Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) devices market is primarily segmented into two types: Digital PCR and Real-time PCR. Digital PCR offers precise quantification of nucleic acids by partitioning the sample, enabling highly sensitive detection and absolute quantification. In contrast, Real-time PCR, also known as quantitative PCR (qPCR), allows for the monitoring of DNA amplification in real-time through fluorescence detection, making it suitable for quantifying RNA and genetic material in various applications, including diagnostics and research.</p></p>
