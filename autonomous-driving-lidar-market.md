<p><p>The Autonomous Driving LiDAR market is categorized into Mechanical, Semi-Solid, Solid-State, and Others. Mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to emit and detect laser beams, offering wide coverage but higher costs and bulk. Semi-Solid LiDAR combines moving parts with limited motion, balancing performance and durability. Solid-State LiDAR has no moving parts, providing compact, reliable, and cost-effective solutions ideal for mass adoption. Others include emerging or hybrid technologies. Each type caters to varying needs in precision, durability, and scalability for autonomous vehicles.</p></p>
