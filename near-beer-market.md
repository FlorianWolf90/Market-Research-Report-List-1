<p><ul><li>Limit Fermentation</li><li>Dealcoholization Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The near beer market includes two primary types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves brewing beer with restricted fermentation, resulting in a low alcohol content, typically below 0.5% ABV. This method maintains a beer-like flavor profile. In contrast, the dealcoholization method involves brewing standard beer and then removing most of the alcohol through various techniques, such as reverse osmosis or evaporation. Both methods aim to provide a beer experience with reduced alcohol content for consumers seeking alternatives.</p></p>
