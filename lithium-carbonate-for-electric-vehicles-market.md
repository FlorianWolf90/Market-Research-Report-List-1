<p><p>Lithium carbonate is a key component in electric vehicle (EV) batteries, enabling efficient energy storage. The market for lithium carbonate can be categorized into two primary types: salt lake extraction and lithium ore extraction. Salt lake extraction involves evaporating brine from salt flats, which is cost-effective and sustainable, but dependent on climatic conditions. In contrast, lithium ore extraction entails mining hard rock deposits, providing higher purity lithium but requiring more intensive and environmentally impactful processing methods. Both methods play crucial roles in supplying lithium for the growing EV market.</p></p>
