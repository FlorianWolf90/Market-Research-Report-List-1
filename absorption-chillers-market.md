<p><p>Absorption chillers are cooling systems that utilize heat sources, such as natural gas, geothermal energy, or solar thermal energy, to provide refrigeration. These chillers operate based on the absorption refrigeration cycle, where a refrigerant is absorbed by a liquid, allowing for effective cooling without the need for mechanical compressors. <p><ul><li>Single Stage</li><li>Double Stage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Absorption chillers are categorized into single-stage and double-stage types based on their cooling efficiency and operational complexity. Single-stage absorption chillers utilize one absorption cycle to produce chilled water, making them suitable for smaller applications. In contrast, double-stage absorption chillers employ two cycles, enhancing their cooling capacity and efficiency, particularly in large-scale industrial settings. The choice between these types depends on factors like required cooling load, energy source, and installation costs, influencing market demand and applications across various sectors.</p></p>
