<p><p>The patient vital signs monitor market is categorized into three segments: high-acuity monitors, mid-acuity monitors, and low-acuity monitors. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients, providing comprehensive data and advanced features for intensive care settings. Mid-acuity monitors are suitable for patients requiring less intensive observation, often used in surgical wards or telemetry units. Low-acuity monitors are utilized in general medical settings, focusing on basic vital signs tracking for stable patients, ensuring efficient and cost-effective patient monitoring across various healthcare environments.</p></p>
