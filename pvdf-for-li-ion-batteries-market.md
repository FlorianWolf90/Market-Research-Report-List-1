<p><p>PVDF (polyvinylidene fluoride) is a critical polymer used in lithium-ion batteries for its excellent chemical resistance and electrochemical stability. The market for PVDF in this context can be segmented into two main types: emulsion polymerization and suspension polymerization. Emulsion polymerization involves producing PVDF through a water-based process, yielding finer particles suited for coatings, while suspension polymerization generates larger particles that enhance mechanical properties. Both methods contribute significantly to the performance and efficiency of battery components, particularly in separators and binders.</p></p>
