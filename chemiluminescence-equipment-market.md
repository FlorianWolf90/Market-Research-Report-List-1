<p><ul><li>Direct Luminescence</li><li>Enzymatic Luminescence</li><li>Electrochemiluminescence</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The chemiluminescence equipment market comprises three primary types: direct luminescence, enzymatic luminescence, and electrochemiluminescence. Direct luminescence involves the emission of light from chemical reactions without requiring a catalyst. Enzymatic luminescence utilizes enzyme-catalyzed reactions to produce light, often applied in biological assays. Electrochemiluminescence combines electrochemical reactions with luminescent properties, enabling precise measurement and detection, commonly used in biosensors. Each type caters to distinct applications in research, diagnostics, and environmental monitoring, enhancing analytical capabilities.</p></p>
