<p><p>The pasta market comprises two primary types: dry pasta and fresh pasta. Dry pasta, typically made from durum wheat, has a longer shelf life and is often favored for its convenience and versatility in cooking. Fresh pasta, on the other hand, is made from higher moisture content ingredients, leading to a softer texture and quicker cooking time, but with a shorter shelf life. Both types cater to different culinary preferences and are essential components of diverse global cuisines.</p></p>
