<p><p>The sorbet stabilizer market comprises various types, each serving unique purposes in the formulation of sorbets. Locust bean gum provides a creamy texture and improves freeze-thaw stability. Guar gum enhances viscosity and mouthfeel. Xanthan gum acts as a thickener, stabilizing ingredients and preventing ice crystal formation. Sodium alginate offers gel-forming properties and is effective in maintaining texture. Carboxymethyl cellulose improves product consistency, while carrageenan helps retain moisture. Other stabilizers may include pectin and tara gum, catering to diverse formulations.</p></p>
